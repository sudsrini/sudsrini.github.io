## Highlights

- Drove root cause investigation on recurring compressor stall failures on the FS7 slush machine; conducted systematic teardowns across 5 affected units to identify witness marks on the compressor rotor indicating debris jamming, coordinated with China supplier for batch-level RCA, and supported an interim software correction to contain the issue
- Owned risk assessment to validate removal of a design feature from the FS7 auger (the mechanical component that scrapes ice off the evaporator barrel and drives slush formation); ran performance trials across key recipes, confirmed design parity on 75% of test cases, and implemented a software adjustment to address remaining gaps
- Built a data analysis tool for processing 73-column raw data logs from frozen treats machines: automated data cleaning, anomaly flagging, and chart generation, cutting analysis time from 15 minutes to under 10 seconds per run, with adoption across the team for multiple product lines
- Devised a mist spray condensation test method as a practical alternative to an unavailable humidity chamber setup: used it to validate prototype designs for condensation management on the FS3, cutting test cycle time by ~80%
- Collaborated across engineering, testing, and product development teams, and coordinated with China counterparts to align testing protocols and share failure findings across domestic and international builds
